### Q16: What is the primary intake for Austrian universities?
The primary intake is the Winter Semester, starting in October. Some courses also admit in the Summer Semester starting in March.

### Q17: Is Austria safe for international students?
Yes, Austria is consistently ranked among the safest countries in the world with very low crime rates.

### Q18: Do I need to speak German to live in Austria?
No, most people speak excellent English, especially in student cities. However, basic German is highly recommended for daily activities and student jobs.

### Q19: How long is a Bachelor's degree in Austria?
Most Bachelor's degrees at public universities last 3 years (6 semesters) and require 180 ECTS credits.

### Q20: How long is a Master's degree in Austria?
Master's degrees typically last 2 years (4 semesters) and require 120 ECTS credits.

### Q21: Are public universities in Austria free?
They are free for EU/EEA citizens. Non-EU students pay a federally regulated fee of €726.72 per semester, which is extremely cheap compared to other destinations.

### Q22: What is a Fachhochschule (FH)?
A Fachhochschule is a University of Applied Sciences, offering practice-oriented, structured education with mandatory internships, as opposed to research-focused traditional public universities.

### Q23: Can I transfer credits from an Indian university to an Austrian university?
Yes, credit transfers are possible if the course syllabus and ECTS equivalents match the Austrian curriculum. The academic department makes the final decision.

### Q24: What is the ECTS system?
ECTS (European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System) is the standard credit system used across Europe. 1 academic year corresponds to 60 ECTS.

### Q25: Can I apply to multiple universities in Austria?
Yes, there is no centralized application system (like Uni-Assist in Germany). You must apply directly to each university individually.

### Q26: What is the climate like in Austria?
Austria has a temperate continental climate with cold winters (ideal for skiing) and warm, pleasant summers.

### Q27: What is the currency of Austria?
Austria uses the Euro (€).

### Q28: Are shops open on Sundays in Austria?
No, by law almost all retail shops and supermarkets are closed on Sundays. Only select train station and airport shops remain open.

### Q29: How do I contact emergency services in Austria?
Euro-emergency number is 112. Fire is 122, Police is 133, and Ambulance is 144.

### Q30: Is the Austrian degree recognized worldwide?
Yes, degrees from accredited Austrian public universities are internationally recognized under the Bologna Process.

### Q31: What is the Special University Entrance Qualification?
It is a mandatory document for non-EU applicants applying to public universities, proving that you are eligible to study the same degree program in your home country (India).

### Q32: Do Universities of Applied Sciences (FHs) require the Special Entrance Qualification?
No, most FHs do not require the Special University Entrance Qualification, making them a popular alternative for international students.

### Q33: What Indian documents need to be legalized for Austria?
All Indian academic certificates (degrees, transcripts, diplomas) and civil documents (birth certificate, police clearance) must be legalized with an Apostille.

### Q34: Where do I get an Apostille in India?
Apostilles are issued by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), Government of India. You can submit documents through authorized outsourced agencies.

### Q35: Do my documents need to be translated?
Yes, if they are not in English or German. If in English, many universities accept them, but the visa authority (MA35) requires German translations for birth and police clearance certificates.

### Q36: Who can translate my documents for Austrian authorities?
Translations must be done by a court-certified translator (in Austria or India) whose signature is verified.

### Q37: What is a provisional degree certificate?
A temporary certificate issued by your university before the final degree convocation. Most Austrian universities accept it for admission, provided it is apostilled.

### Q38: Does Austria require an APS certificate for Indian students?
No. Unlike Germany, Austria does NOT require an APS certificate for Indian student visa applications.

### Q39: What is the minimum GPA required for admission?
Generally, a minimum GPA equivalent to 2.5 on the Austrian scale (~60% average in India) is required, but competitive programs require higher grades.

### Q40: Can I apply with a 3-year Bachelor's degree from India?
Yes, if the ECTS credits and core subjects match the prerequisites of the Austrian Master's curriculum.

### Q41: Is IELTS mandatory for English-taught programs?
Yes, most universities require proof of English proficiency. IELTS Academic 6.5 is the standard requirement.

### Q42: Do public universities accept TOEFL?
Yes, a TOEFL iBT score of 87 or higher is widely accepted.

### Q43: Is Duolingo English Test accepted in Austria?
No, public universities in Austria do not accept Duolingo. Stick to IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE.

### Q44: Can I get an English medium of instruction (MOI) waiver?
Rarely. Most public universities require standard test scores (IELTS/TOEFL) regardless of your previous medium of instruction.

### Q45: What is the PTE score required for Austria?
A minimum score of 62 (Academic) is generally accepted for English programs.

### Q46: Are LORs (Letters of Recommendation) required for Masters?
Yes, most Master programs require 2 academic or professional letters of recommendation.

### Q47: How long should my Statement of Purpose (SOP) be?
An SOP should be 1-2 pages, detailing your academic background, motivation for the course, and why you chose Austria.

### Q48: Do I need to send physical documents by post for admission?
Yes, for most public universities (like TU Wien), after online pre-registration, you must mail certified, legalized paper documents by post.

### Q49: How long does it take for a university to review my application?
Equivalence and admission reviews typically take 6 to 12 weeks after they receive your physical documents.

### Q50: What is the general admission period?
It is the main period where applications are open. Non-EU students must apply before July 15 (Winter) or January 15 (Summer).

### Q51: What is the extension period?
A secondary enrollment window mostly for EU citizens. Non-EU students cannot use this because they won't get their visa in time.

### Q52: Can I apply if my final semester results are pending?
Yes, you can apply with transcripts up to the penultimate semester, but you must submit the final degree before official enrollment.

### Q53: What is a Zulassungsbescheid?
It is the official Letter of Admission issued by an Austrian university once your application is approved.

### Q54: How is the Zulassungsbescheid delivered?
It is sent to your home address via registered international mail (post), or made available digitally in the university portal.

### Q55: Can I track the status of my university application?
Yes, through the university's online service portal (TISS, u-space, LFUonline, etc.).

### Q56: How much is the tuition fee per semester for non-EU students?
For public universities, it is €726.72 per semester.

### Q57: What is the ÖH-Beitrag?
It is the mandatory Student Union representation fee, which is €24.70 per semester for 2026.

### Q58: Do I have to pay tuition fees during a leave of absence?
No, if you take an approved leave of absence, you are exempt from tuition fees but may still need to pay the ÖH-Beitrag.

### Q59: Are there application fees for Austrian public universities?
No, public universities do not charge any application processing fees.

### Q60: How much do Universities of Applied Sciences (FHs) cost?
Tuition fees at FHs range from €726.72 to €1,500 per semester for non-EU students. MCI Innsbruck is higher (€6,250/semester).

### Q61: Are tuition fees refundable if my visa is rejected?
Yes, if you notify the university before the semester starts and prove your visa was rejected, you can request a tuition refund.

### Q62: When must I pay the semester tuition fee?
Fees must be paid during the enrollment/registration period, before the semester starts.

### Q63: Can I pay my tuition fee in installments?
No, the semester tuition fee must be paid in full as a single transaction.

### Q64: Do PhD students pay tuition fees in Austria?
At public universities, PhD students are often exempt from tuition fees for the minimum duration of their study plus two extra semesters.

### Q65: What is the tuition fee for German language preparation courses?
If you are admitted with a German condition, you join the Vorstudienlehrgang (VdU) which costs around €1,200 – €1,400 per semester.

### Q66: How much is the student health insurance fee in 2026?
The ÖGK student self-insurance is €69.13 per month in 2026.

### Q67: Is the Student Union fee (ÖH-Beitrag) refundable?
No, the ÖH-Beitrag is non-refundable.

### Q68: Are there private universities in Austria?
Yes, but they are expensive, with tuition fees ranging from €5,000 to €20,000+ per year.

### Q69: Can I get a tuition waiver based on good grades?
Yes, public universities offer performance scholarships (Leistungsstipendium) that can refund your tuition if you maintain a high GPA.

### Q70: Are Indian banks accepted for student loans to Austria?
Yes, nationalized banks (SBI, Bank of Baroda) and private lenders (ICICI, Avanse, Credila) are accepted for proof of funds.

### Q71: What is the Student Residence Permit called in Austria?
It is called 'Aufenthaltsbewilligung - Student' (Residence Permit - Student).

### Q72: Where do I apply for the Austrian Student Residence Permit?
You must apply in person at the Austrian Embassy in New Delhi, or through VFS Global centers if authorized.

### Q73: What is the difference between Visa D and Residence Permit?
Visa D is a 4-month travel visa issued for entry. The Residence Permit is a 1-year plastic card collected after you arrive in Austria.

### Q74: How much money do I need for the visa if I am under 24?
For 2026, you need at least €680.12 per month, which totals €8,161.44 for a 12-month permit.

### Q75: How much money do I need for the visa if I am 24 or older?
For 2026, you need at least €1,230.15 per month, which totals €14,761.80 for a 12-month permit.

### Q76: What is the rent deduction offset for visa funds?
The default calculation assumes a rent of €380.00. If your actual monthly rent is higher, you must add the excess amount to your bank savings.

### Q77: Does Austria require a blocked account like Germany?
No, Austria does not require a blocked account. You can keep the funds in a standard Indian savings account, provided you can prove origin.

### Q78: What is proof of origin of funds?
Documentation proving where your visa savings came from (e.g., parents' tax returns, salary slips, provident fund withdrawals, or loan sanction letters).

### Q79: Can a relative sponsor my studies in Austria?
Yes, a sponsor can sign a Declaration of Sponsorship (Haftungserklärung), but they must prove sufficient regular income and undergo financial checks.

### Q80: How long does it take to get the Student Residence Permit?
The official processing time is 3 to 4 months. Apply as soon as you receive your Zulassungsbescheid.

### Q81: Can I track my visa application status?
You can contact the embassy or the local authority in Austria (MA35 for Vienna) handling your file via email/phone.

### Q82: What happens after my residence permit is approved?
The embassy in India will contact you to submit your passport and travel insurance, and issue a 4-month Visa D.

### Q83: Do I need to submit a Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) for the visa?
Yes, a legalized (Apostilled) PCC from the Passport Office is mandatory, and it must be less than 3 months old at submission.

### Q84: Does my PCC need to be translated into German?
Yes, the visa authority in Austria requires the PCC to be translated into German by a certified translator.

### Q85: What is travel insurance requirements for Visa D?
It must cover at least €30,000, have zero deductible, cover repatriation, and be valid for the first 1-2 months of your stay.

### Q86: How do I get my physical Residence Permit card in Austria?
Within 3 days of arrival, register your address (Meldezettel), then visit the local migration authority (e.g., MA35 in Vienna) to collect the card.

### Q87: How much does the Residence Permit card cost?
A total of €160, payable in stages (€120 at application, €20 at approval, €20 card fee).

### Q88: How long is the Student Residence Permit card valid?
It is valid for 12 months and must be renewed annually in Austria before it expires.

### Q89: What are the renewal requirements for the Residence Permit?
You must show proof of study progress (at least 16 ECTS credits or 8 semester hours completed in the previous academic year) and updated financial funds.

### Q90: What happens if I fail to complete 16 ECTS in a year?
Your residence permit renewal may be rejected, and you would have to leave Austria. Ensure you pass your exams!

### Q91: How do I find accommodation in Austria?
You can apply for student dormitories through OeAD Student Housing, STUWO, or Akademikerhilfe, or search for shared apartments (WGs) on WG-Gesucht.

### Q92: What is OeAD Student Housing?
An official non-profit organization providing fully furnished, utility-included student rooms across Austria, popular for international arrivals.

### Q93: Do I need a rental contract before applying for the visa?
Yes, a legally binding accommodation contract is a mandatory document for the visa application.

### Q94: What is a Meldezettel?
It is the official certificate of residence registration. You must get this from the local Meldeamt within 3 days of arrival.

### Q95: How do I register my address in Austria?
Take your signed registration form (Meldezettel) and your passport to the local municipal office (Rathaus or Bezirksamt). Registration is free.

### Q96: How much does student dorm accommodation cost?
Dorm rooms typically cost €300 to €550 per month, depending on the city and room type (single/shared).

### Q97: What is the deposit for student housing?
Most dorms require a security deposit of 1 to 3 months' rent before move-in, which is refunded upon departure.

### Q98: Can I rent a room in a private apartment (WG)?
Yes, WG rooms are very popular. You will sign a sub-lease agreement (Untermietvertrag) which is valid for the visa.

### Q99: How do I buy a SIM card in Austria?
You can buy SIM cards at supermarkets (like HoT at Hofer) or electronic stores. You must register it with a photo ID (passport) before activation.

### Q100: How do I open a student bank account?
Visit a local bank branch ( Erste Bank, Raiffeisen) with your passport, university enrollment letter, and Meldezettel.

### Q101: Are student bank accounts free?
Yes, most major banks in Austria offer free checking accounts for students under 27 years.

### Q102: How do I get the ÖGK student health insurance?
After official university enrollment, submit the application form, passport copy, and enrollment confirmation to the local ÖGK office.

### Q103: Is the student health insurance valid in other EU countries?
Yes, the ÖGK card contains the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) on the back, covering emergency treatment in all EU/EEA countries.

### Q104: What is the cost of public transport for students?
Vienna offers a student semester ticket for €75 (approx.). Other cities offer similar student semester tickets starting at €90.

### Q105: Can I drive in Austria with an Indian driving license?
An Indian license is valid for up to 6 months after arrival. For longer stays, you must convert it to an Austrian license, which requires a practical driving test.

### Q106: Are international students allowed to work in Austria?
Yes, non-EU students can work part-time for up to 20 hours per week.

### Q107: What permit is required to work part-time?
A Beschäftigungsbewilligung (work permit) is required. The employer must apply for this at the AMS on your behalf.

### Q108: How long does the AMS take to issue a work permit?
Typically 2 to 4 weeks. Work cannot begin until the permit is officially granted.

### Q109: Can I work full-time during university holidays?
No, the 20-hour-per-week limit applies year-round for student visa holders, including during summer and winter holidays.

### Q110: Can I do a paid internship in Austria?
Yes. If the internship is a mandatory part of your curriculum (Pflichtpraktikum), it is exempt from standard AMS work permit rules but must be reported to the AMS.

### Q111: What is the tax-free allowance for student work in 2026?
Students can earn up to €12,792 per year tax-free in 2026.

### Q112: What is the Geringfügig limits in 2026?
Income up to ~€518.44 per month is considered marginal employment (Geringfügige Beschäftigung) and is exempt from standard pension/health insurance taxes.

### Q113: Can I work as a freelancer (Selbstständig) in Austria?
No, the Student Residence Permit does not allow self-employment or freelancing unless you apply for a specific change of status.

### Q114: What is the post-study work visa in Austria?
Upon graduation, you can apply for a 12-month Residence Permit for Job Search (Jobsuche-Visum) to look for a qualified job.

### Q115: What is the Red-White-Red Card?
The RWR Card (Rot-Weiß-Rot-Karte) is a point-based work permit for qualified non-EU citizens, granting residence and work rights for a specific employer.

### Q116: How does an international graduate get the RWR Card?
If you find a job matching your university degree that pays the minimum collective agreement wage (Kollektivvertrag) for that position, you can apply for the RWR Card for Graduates.

### Q117: Is there a minimum wage requirement for the graduate RWR Card?
Yes, you must be paid the local collective agreement minimum wage for graduates in that position. There is no longer a fixed statutory minimum threshold since the 2022 reforms.

### Q118: Does the graduate RWR card require a labor market test?
No, university graduates applying for the RWR Card are exempt from the regional labor market test (Ersatzkraftprüfung).

### Q119: What is the RWR Card Plus?
After holding the standard RWR Card for 2 years, you can transition to the RWR Card Plus, which gives you unrestricted access to the Austrian labor market.

### Q120: Can I apply for permanent residency in Austria?
Yes, after 5 years of legal, continuous residency in Austria (time spent on a student permit counts as 50%), you can apply for Long-Term Resident EU status.
