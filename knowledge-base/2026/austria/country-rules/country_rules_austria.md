# Country Rules and Guidelines for Students in Austria (2026)

## Overview
Living and studying in Austria requires strict adherence to federal and local regulations. This document details the key rules that international students must follow in 2026.

---

## 1. Part-Time Work Rules
- **Work Hours:** All international students holding a Student Residence Permit are legally allowed to work up to **20 hours per week**.
- **Work Permit (Beschäftigungsbewilligung):** Your employer must apply for this permit at the AMS (Public Employment Service) before you start working. Since 2024, the AMS grants this automatically without checking if an Austrian citizen is available for the job (no labor market test).
- **Minimum Wage:** There is no universal federal minimum wage in Austria. Instead, minimum wages are set by collective bargaining agreements (Kollektivvertrag) for each industry, typically ranging from **€10.50 to €15.00 per hour** in 2026.

## 2. Tax & Social Insurance Rules
- **Tax-Free Income Limit:** Students can earn up to **€12,792 per year** tax-free in 2026.
- **Social Security Contributions:** Income above ~€518.44 per month (Geringfügigkeitsgrenze in 2026) is subject to mandatory pension and health insurance contributions, deducted automatically from your paycheck.

## 3. Accommodation & Address Registration (Meldezettel)
- **Meldepflicht:** By law, you must register your residential address at the local municipal office (Meldeamt / Magistratisches Bezirksamt) within **3 days** of moving into any accommodation.
- **Required Document:** You need a signed "Meldezettel" form, co-signed by your landlord or dorm manager.
- **Address Change:** Any change of flat requires a new registration within 3 days.

## 4. Student Bank Account
- **Access:** To receive salaries, pay rent, and show funds for residence permit renewals, you need a Euro bank account.
- **Popular Banks:** Erste Bank, Raiffeisenbank, Bank Austria, and N26 (digital bank). Most banks offer free student accounts (Studentenkonto) up to age 27.

## 5. SIM Card Registration
- **Identity Check:** Under Austrian security laws, all SIM cards (both prepaid and contract) must be registered with a photo ID check before activation.
- **Popular Providers:** HoT (cheap, available at Hofer), Drei, Magenta, A1, yesss!.

## 6. Post-Study Work & PR Pathway (Red-White-Red Card)
- **Job Search Period:** Upon graduation from an Austrian university (Master/PhD or Bachelor), you can apply for a **12-month job search residence permit**.
- **Red-White-Red Card (RWR Karte):** If you find a job matching your qualifications that pays the statutory minimum wage (regulated by the collective agreement / Kollektivvertrag), you can transition to the Red-White-Red Card for Graduates. This is a point-based work permit leading to permanent residency (EU Blue Card or Red-White-Red Card Plus).
