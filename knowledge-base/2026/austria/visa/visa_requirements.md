# Student Visa and Residence Permit for Austria (2026)

## Overview
For stays exceeding 6 months, international students (from third-party countries like India) require a **Student Residence Permit** (Aufenthaltsbewilligung - Student). For entering Austria to receive the permit, a **Visa D** is issued by the Austrian Embassy after the residence permit application is pre-approved in Austria.

---

## Key Specifications

### 1. Visa Type
- **Aufenthaltsbewilligung - Student (Residence Permit Student):** For stays longer than 6 months. Valid for 12 months, renewable annually in Austria.
- **Visa D (National Visa):** A 4-month travel visa issued by the Embassy in New Delhi once the residence permit is approved, allowing you to enter Austria and collect your residence permit card.

### 2. Eligibility
- Valid Letter of Admission (Zulassungsbescheid) from a recognized Austrian university or UAS.
- Proof of secure accommodation in Austria.
- Proof of health insurance (travel insurance for travel/first month, then ÖGK student self-insurance).
- Proof of sufficient financial funds.
- Clean criminal record.

### 3. Required Funds (2026 Subsistence Rates)
To secure the residence permit, you must prove you have sufficient monthly funds to cover your living costs. Under Austrian law, the required amounts are updated annually:
- **Students under 24 years of age:** €680.12 per month (Minimum €8,161.44 for 12 months).
- **Students aged 24 and older:** €1,230.15 per month (Minimum €14,761.80 for 12 months).
- **Additional Rent Offset:** If your monthly rent exceeds €380.00, you must prove additional funds covering the difference.
- **Example calculation:** If you are 25 years old and your rent is €480/month:
  Required funds = €1,230.15 + (€480.00 - €380.00) = €1,330.15 per month.

### 4. Proof of Funds / Blocked Account Status
*Unlike Germany, Austria does NOT require a formal 'blocked account' (Sperrkonto).* Instead, you must prove availability of funds using:
- **Savings Account Statement:** A bank account statement in the student's name from a recognized bank in India (with transaction history of last 6 months, proving clean source of funds).
- **Declaration of Sponsorship (Haftungserklärung):** A declaration of liability from a sponsor residing in the EU who has sufficient income.
- **Educational Loan:** Confirmation of loan disbursement from an approved bank.
- **Scholarship:** Official award letter covering the required monthly amount.
- **Proof of Origin of Funds:** You must explain where the money came from (e.g., parents' income tax returns, salary slips, property sale documents). Austrian authorities are extremely strict about checking the source of funds to prevent money laundering.

### 5. Processing Time
- Typically **3 to 4 months** from the date of submission. Students must apply as early as possible.

### 6. Interview & Biometrics
- Submitted in person at the Austrian Embassy in New Delhi (or VFS Global centers). Biometrics (fingerprints and photo) are collected during submission.

### 7. Part-time Work Hours
- Holders of a Student Residence Permit are legally allowed to work part-time for up to **20 hours per week**. This requires a work permit (Beschäftigungsbewilligung) issued to the employer by the Austrian Public Employment Service (AMS), which is granted automatically without a labor market test.
